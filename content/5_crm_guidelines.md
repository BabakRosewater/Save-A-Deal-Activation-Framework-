# CRM Guidelines — Salesperson & Manager Notes

> If it’s not in CRM, it didn’t happen.

High-quality notes are the fuel for effective Save-A-Deal meetings and follow-up.

---

## Salesperson CRM Notes — Required Fields

**Suggested Format:**

> **MOTIVATOR:**  
> Why they’re in the market; what problem they’re solving.  
>  
> **VEHICLES CONSIDERED:**  
> What they drove / liked / eliminated and why.  
>  
> **NUMBERS PRESENTED:**  
> Price, payment, down, term, trade details, if applicable.  
>  
> **OBJECTIONS / CONCERNS:**  
> What they hesitated about (payment, timing, lender, spouse, etc.).  
>  
> **PROMISE MADE:**  
> What I’m sending and by when (e.g., towing specs + 2 payment options by 12:00 p.m. tomorrow).  
>  
> **TIMELINE:**  
> When they hope to decide (this weekend, next 2–3 weeks, etc.).

---

## Manager CRM Notes — Required Fields

**Suggested Format:**

> **MGR INTRO:**  
> When and how I met the guest; what I reinforced (no pressure, transparency, etc.).  
>  
> **MGR READ:**  
> - Motivator and urgency  
> - Who is involved in the decision  
> - Deal temperature (Hot / Warm / Cool)  
>  
> **MGR COMMITMENTS:**  
> Any promises I made (review structure, sharpen pencil, explore lender options, etc.).  
>  
> **NEXT-DAY STRATEGY:**  
> Recommended talk track and plan for Save-A-Deal follow-up.

---

## Best Practices

- Write notes as if someone else will need to pick up the deal tomorrow.
- Avoid vague language (“talked numbers,” “shopping around”) – be specific.
- Always restate **promises** and **timelines** clearly.
- Keep tone professional, respectful, and fact-based.
